Cuban Cigars

Cuba are globally known for their cigars, which come at a high price (even in Cuba)! The 'Cohiba' brand is the most popular as they were famously smoked by Fidel Castro. Each cigar sold is rolled by hand and fake cigars are identified by details in the packaging.

Did you know?

Cuba is populated with 11 million people, making it the largest island in the Caribbean! The shape of the island is said to resemble an alligator and is often referred to in Spanish as "El Cocodrilo" or "El Caimá".

Cuba’s flag is made up of three blue horizontal stripes, a red triangle on the left and a white star.The three blue stripes refer to the three divisions the island once had. The two white stripes between the blue refer to the strength for their own independence. The red triangle represents the struggle of obtaining equality and freedom of the people of Cuba and the bloodshed for this. The white star symbolizes the complete freedom of the people.

Another fact!

Cuba became a colony of Spain. In 1902, Cuba became a de factor United States protectorate following Spanish–American War of 1898.
